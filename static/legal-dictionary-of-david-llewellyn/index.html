Legal Dictionary of David Llewellyn

09 November 2016
A collection of words coined by David Llewellyn and encouraged to be used when working in areas of law and public policy.


Chawelcome n. A feeling or sense of appreciation, used to convey gratitude.

Fizza n. A person who makes big promises and delivers nothing.

Shituation n. A situation that is out of control, seemingly beyond repair.

Wahzoo n. A state of disarray, often articulated with the phrase “up the wahzoo”.
